Orange Chicken Recipe:

Orange chicken is a popular Chinese-American dish known for its sweet and tangy flavor profile. Typically made with bite-sized pieces of chicken that are battered and fried until crispy, then coated in a sticky orange-flavored sauce, it offers a delightful combination of savory and citrusy notes. The sauce is made with ingredients like fresh orange juice, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and brown sugar, creating a harmonious balance of sweet and savory flavors with a hint of tanginess. Garnished with sesame seeds and green onions for added flavor and presentation, orange chicken is often served over steamed rice or alongside stir-fried vegetables for a complete meal that satisfies both the palate and the appetite. To make orange chicken at home, start by marinating bite-sized pieces of chicken breast in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic powder, and ginger for about 30 minutes. In a separate bowl, prepare the batter by combining flour, cornstarch, salt, and pepper, then dip the marinated chicken pieces into the batter and fry them until golden brown and crispy. Meanwhile, prepare the sauce by simmering orange juice, soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, and grated orange zest until it thickens slightly. Once the chicken is cooked, toss it in the orange sauce until evenly coated, then garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions before serving. Enjoy this homemade version of orange chicken with steamed rice or your favorite stir-fried vegetables for a delicious and satisfying meal.

    Ingredents
  • 1 pound (about 450g) boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon salt
